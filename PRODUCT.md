# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters scanning between meetings, hiring managers and tech leads doing deeper reviews, potential freelance clients evaluating fit, and peers in the frontend community checking craft. They arrive on mobile and desktop with varying time and attention — some need a fast signal, others want proof of depth.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Ray Fajar Salinggih, Frontend Engineer, that convinces visitors to take action: reach out, download the CV, or explore projects. Success means visitors immediately grasp Vue.js / Nuxt.js / TypeScript expertise, see evidence of engineering craft (architecture, design systems, enterprise work), and trust Ray as someone worth interviewing or hiring.

## Brand Personality

Technical and bold — sharp contrast, intentional motion, and editorial confidence that shows engineering depth without decoration for its own sake. Voice is direct and craft-forward: the site itself is a proof of frontend quality. Reference direction: editorial developer portfolios with strong typography and magazine-like hierarchy.

## Anti-references

Over-designed, flashy portfolios: heavy gratuitous animation, glassmorphism, gradient text, decorative effects that obscure the work. The interface should feel bold through structure and precision, not visual noise.

## Design Principles

1. **Show depth through craft** — typography, layout, and performance demonstrate frontend skill; effects earn their place.
2. **Editorial confidence** — hierarchy and type carry authority; content is scannable yet substantive.
3. **Bold but intentional** — contrast and motion signal technical boldness, never spectacle.
4. **Mixed-audience clarity** — recruiters get a fast read; hiring managers and clients can go deeper into experience and projects.
5. **Practice what you preach** — the portfolio is itself a case study in Vue/Nuxt engineering quality.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AAA where practical: strict contrast ratios, full keyboard navigation, semantic HTML, visible focus states, and `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all animation.
